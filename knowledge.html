FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is NIPT?
Non-invasive prenatal testing analyzes cell-free fetal DNA in the maternal bloodstream to estimate the risk of certain chromosomal abnormalities — without any procedural risk to the pregnancy.
From which week of pregnancy can the test be performed?
From 8 weeks of gestation, when sufficient fetal DNA is present in maternal circulation.
Is the test safe?
Yes — it requires only a single 10 mL maternal blood draw, with no risk to mother or baby.
How long do results take?
Typically 3–5 working days from sample receipt for standard panels.
Is NIPTunegx® diagnostic?
No. It is a high-sensitivity screening test. A high-risk result should be confirmed with a diagnostic procedure such as CVS or amniocentesis.
Can the test be used in twin or IVF pregnancies?
Yes — NIPTunegx® is suitable for singleton, twin and IVF pregnancies. Reporting accounts for the specifics of each scenario.
Can NIPTunegx® detect fetal sex?
Yes — fetal sex disclosure is offered as an optional add-on, subject to local regulations.
Who should not take the test?
Patients who have had organ transplants, active or recent cancer, known maternal chromosomal imbalances, recent heterologous transfusions, or maternal Turner syndrome.
