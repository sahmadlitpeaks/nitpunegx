Patient suitability

Who is the right candidate?

Use this clinical guidance to identify when NIPTunegx® is highly recommended, suitable, or contraindicated.

Highly recommended

  • Advanced maternal age (>35 years)
  • Advanced paternal age (>40 years)
  • Ultrasound abnormalities
  • Family history of genetic disorders
  • Abnormal first-trimester combined test

Suitable

  • Singleton pregnancies
  • Twin pregnancies
  • IVF / assisted reproduction
  • Donor-egg pregnancies
  • Patients seeking reassurance

Not suitable

  • Organ-transplant recipients
  • Active or recent maternal cancer
  • Maternal chromosomal imbalances
  • Recent heterologous transfusion
  • Maternal Turner syndrome

Frequently asked

Common questions, clearly answered

From which week of pregnancy can the test be performed?
NIPTunegx® can be performed from 8 weeks of gestation, once enough cell-free fetal DNA is present in the maternal bloodstream.
Is the test safe for the mother and baby?
Yes. The test only requires a single 10 mL maternal blood sample — there is no procedural risk to the pregnancy, unlike invasive options such as amniocentesis or CVS.
How long does it take to receive results?
Most results are returned in 3 to 5 working days from the moment the laboratory receives the sample.
Is NIPTunegx® diagnostic?
NIPT is a high-sensitivity screening test, not a diagnostic test. A positive or high-risk result should be confirmed by an invasive diagnostic procedure such as CVS or amniocentesis.
Can the test be used in twin or IVF pregnancies?
Yes — NIPTunegx® is suitable for singleton, twin and IVF pregnancies. The reporting account for the specifics of each scenario.
